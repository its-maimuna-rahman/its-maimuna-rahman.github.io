Academics

B.S. in Applied Statistics and Data Science
Institute of Applied Statistics and Data Science, University of Dhaka

First Year (30 Credits)

  • Data science lifecycle, big data sources, and data governance
  • Descriptive vs. inferential statistics; variables and scales of measurement
  • Experimental and non-experimental study designs; sample surveys and questionnaires
  • Data cleaning, frequency distributions, histograms, ogives, and box-plots
  • Measures of location, dispersion, skewness, kurtosis; three- and five-number summaries
  • Bivariate data: contingency tables, association measures, correlation, simple linear regression
  • Data visualization and analysis using spreadsheet tools (Excel, Google Sheets)
  • Combinatorial analysis: permutations, combinations, counting principles
  • Axioms of probability, sample spaces, conditional probability, Bayes' formula, independence
  • Discrete random variables: expectation, variance, Bernoulli, binomial, Poisson, geometric, negative binomial, hypergeometric
  • Continuous random variables: normal, exponential; normal approximation to binomial
  • Joint distributions, independent random variables, sums of random variables, conditional distributions
  • Covariance, correlation, conditional expectation, moment generating functions, probability generating functions
  • Basic Algebra: Unique factorization, congruences, Euler's phi-function, inequalities (Weierstrass, Chebyshev, Cauchy), complex numbers, finite series, theory of equations, beta and gamma functions, Dirichlet's theorem
  • Differential Equations: First-order equations, singular solutions, linear equations with constant coefficients, undetermined coefficients, variation of parameters
  • Discrete Mathematics: Counting principles, inclusion-exclusion, pigeonhole principle, generating functions, recurrence relations, graph theory (trees, connectivity, Eulerian/Hamiltonian graphs, planar graphs), shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra, Floyd-Warshall), spanning trees (Kruskal, Prim)
  • Functions and graphs: polynomial, rational, logarithmic, exponential, trigonometric, hyperbolic, and inverse functions
  • Limits, continuity, and basic limit theorems; properties of continuous functions
  • Differentiation: rules, successive differentiation, Leibnitz theorem, related rates, linear approximations, differentials
  • Mean value theorems (Rolle, Lagrange, Cauchy); extrema, maxima/minima problems, concavity, inflection points
  • Indeterminate forms and L'Hôpital's rule; Taylor and Maclaurin series with remainder forms
  • Vector-valued functions, arc length parameterization, unit tangent/normal/binormal vectors, curvature
  • Partial differentiation: limits, continuity, partial derivatives, chain rule, directional derivatives, gradients, tangent planes, extrema of multivariable functions, Lagrange multipliers
  • Indefinite integrals: integration by parts, substitution, rational functions, reduction formulas
  • Definite integrals: Riemann sums, fundamental theorems of calculus, basic properties
  • Applications: plane areas, solids of revolution, cylindrical shells, cross-sections, arc length, surface area
  • Improper integrals; gamma and beta functions
  • Polar coordinates: area and arc length
  • Double integrals: nonrectangular regions, polar coordinates, surface area, parametric surfaces
  • Triple integrals: cylindrical and spherical coordinates, centers of gravity, change of variables, Jacobians
  • Vector calculus: gradient, divergence, curl, line integrals, Green's theorem
  • Matrices and vectors: operations, trace, determinant, adjoint, inverse, Kronecker product
  • Linear systems: Gaussian elimination, Gauss-Jordan, homogeneous systems, null spaces, rank, generalized inverses
  • Vector spaces: subspaces, linear independence, basis, dimension, inner product, norms, orthogonality, Gram-Schmidt
  • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors: characteristic polynomial, eigenspaces, diagonalization
  • Singular value decomposition (SVD) and Jordan canonical form
  • Quadratic forms: positive/nonnegative definiteness, Sylvester's law of inertia, simultaneous diagonalization
  • Demand and supply theory; elasticity; equilibrium analysis
  • Consumer behavior: utility theory, indifference curves, consumer surplus
  • Production theory: production functions, returns to scale, Cobb-Douglas and CES functions, cost theory
  • Market structures: perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic competition; short-run and long-run equilibrium
  • Factor markets: wage determination, labor supply, interest and capital, present value, real vs. nominal rates
  • Macroeconomics: GDP, NDP, GNP, inflation, consumption and saving functions, investment determinants
  • Economic growth theories: classical (Smith, Malthus), neoclassical growth model
  • Index numbers: construction methods (Laspeyre, Paasche, Fisher, Marshall-Edgeworth), tests, base shifting, CPI applications
  • Reading: Comprehension strategies, skimming/scanning, identifying facts and themes, critical reading, vocabulary building, formal vs. informal writing
  • Writing: Paragraph structure, summarizing, paraphrasing, synthesizing, sentence variety, grammar revision, common errors, academic referencing, essay and email writing
  • Listening: Understanding news, dialogues, interviews, lectures; note-taking; identifying implied meaning
  • Speaking: Everyday communication, introductions, debates, conversations, presentation skills, pronunciation (IPA, vowels, diphthongs)
  • Programming fundamentals: algorithms, flowcharts, levels of programming, compilation process
  • C basics: data types, variables, constants, operators, expressions, control statements (conditional, loops)
  • Functions: return statements, arguments, pointers to functions
  • Arrays: single and multi-dimensional, arrays and pointers, dynamic allocation, initialization
  • Applications in data analysis: frequency distributions, summary statistics, matrix operations, linear regression fitting, sorting
  • All concepts also implemented in C⌄⌄
  • Database fundamentals: MySQL setup, data types, table creation, populating and modifying tables
  • Core SQL: SELECT, FROM, WHERE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY, HAVING clauses
  • Advanced queries: joins, subqueries, common table expressions (CTEs)
  • Functions: aggregation, window functions, date/time functions, user-defined functions (UDFs)
  • Statistical SQL: summary statistics, frequency distributions, correlation, regression analysis
  • Comprehensive oral examination by a committee of selected faculty members
  • Assessment of understanding across all first-year courses
  • Evaluation of communication skills and ability to articulate statistical concepts
  • Demonstration of problem-solving ability in real-time questioning

Second Year (34 Credits)

  • Generating function techniques: moment generating function, cumulant generating function, probability generating function, characteristic function
  • Finding distributions of functions of random variables: change of variable technique, distribution function technique, moment-generating function technique
  • Probability integral transformation; statistic and sampling distribution; law of large numbers; central limit theorem
  • Exact distribution of sample mean; chi-square distribution and its properties; F-distribution and its properties; t-distribution and its properties
  • Non-central chi-square, F and t distributions: definition and derivation
  • Concept of order statistics, distributions of single order statistics, and joint distribution of two or more order statistics
  • Review of probability distributions: uniform, normal, exponential, gamma, beta, log-normal, Cauchy; truncated distributions; compound and mixture distributions; Pearsonian family of distributions
  • Role of insurance in the economy; role of an actuary
  • Fundamentals of theory of interest: simple and compound interest, accumulated value factors, present value factors
  • Effective and nominal rates of interest and discount; equations of value; annuities (immediate, due, deferred, variable)
  • Capital redemption policies; amortization schedules and sinking funds
  • Actuarial mathematics: discrete life annuity, present values of different life annuities, life assurance
  • Premiums: net premiums, office premiums, prospective policy values
  • The basic deterministic model: cash flows, discount functions, balances and reserves
  • Stochastic interest-rate models: basic model for one stochastic interest rate, independent and dependent annual interest rates
  • Fundamental ideas of statistical inference; parametric and non-parametric inference; estimators, statistics, parameters
  • Estimation: method of moments, method of least squares, method of maximum likelihood; properties of MLEs
  • Properties of good point estimators: mean squared error, efficiency, Cramer-Rao lower bound
  • Sufficiency: sufficient statistics, exponential family, factorization theorem, Rao-Blackwell theorem, minimal sufficient statistics, completeness
  • Interval estimation: pivotal quantity, exact and approximate confidence intervals, selecting sample size, simultaneous confidence intervals
  • Hypothesis testing: Neyman-Pearson approach, Fisher approach, Jeffreys' approach
  • Common tests based on normal distribution: one-sample, two-sample (independent and paired), more than two samples
  • Power of tests, Neyman-Pearson lemma, UMP tests, composite hypotheses, generalized likelihood ratio test
  • Goodness-of-fit tests: probability plots, chi-square, Kolmogorov-Smirnov; tests for categorical data
  • Strategy of experimentation; basic principles; guidelines for designing experiments
  • Single factor experiments: ANOVA, fixed effects model, model adequacy checking, estimation of parameters
  • Comparisons among treatment means: contrasts, orthogonal contrasts, multiple testing, Scheffe's method, comparing pairs of means
  • Determining sample size; regression approach to ANOVA
  • Randomized complete block designs (RCBD): statistical analysis, model adequacy checking, estimating model parameters
  • Latin square design; Graeco-Latin square design
  • Balanced incomplete block design (BIBD): statistical analysis, least squares estimation, recovery of intra-block information
  • Basic concepts of demography; sources of demographic data: census, vital registration, sample surveys, population registers
  • Errors in demographic data: types, quality checking, post-enumeration check (PEC)
  • Fertility measures: crude birth rate, ASFR, GFR, TFR, GRR, NRR, child-woman ratio; fecundity and fertility trends
  • Demographic theory: transition theory, Malthus' theory and criticism
  • Mortality measures: CDR, ASDR, infant mortality, child mortality, neonatal mortality; standardized death rates
  • Nuptiality: marriage types, age at marriage, celibacy, widowhood, divorce effects on fertility
  • Migration: internal and international, sources, factors, laws of migration, impact on population growth and economy
  • Graduation of data; life tables: complete and abridged, multiple decrement, working life table, model life tables (Coale-Demeny)
  • Stable and stationary populations
  • Concept of sampling, probability and non-probability sampling, selection and inclusion probability, sampling weight
  • Simple random sampling (SRS): estimation of mean, total, proportion, ratio; unbiasedness, variances, confidence intervals, finite population correction
  • Systematic sampling: sample selection, estimators, comparison with SRS, linear trend and periodic variation
  • Stratified random sampling: formation of strata, estimators, allocation techniques (proportional, Neyman, optimum), comparison with SRS, design effect
  • Auxiliary information: ratio estimators, product estimator, regression estimator; properties and applications in stratified sampling
  • Cluster sampling: formation and size of clusters, equal-sized clusters, comparison with SRS, optimum cluster size
  • Special designs: capture-recapture sampling (Peterson and Chapman estimators), ranked set sampling
  • Interpolation and inverse interpolation: Newton's forward/backward, Lagrange's formula
  • Numerical integration: Simpson's rule, Weddle's rule, trapezoidal rule, Gauss's quadratic formulae
  • Solution of algebraic and transcendental equations: false position, Newton-Raphson method for simultaneous equations
  • Fourier series: periodic functions, Dirichlet conditions, odd/even functions, half-range series, Parseval's identity
  • Laplace transform: elementary functions, properties, initial and final value theorems, inverse Laplace transform, convolution theorem
  • Applications of Laplace transforms in solving differential equations and integrals
  • Introduction to Taylor's and Laurent series
  • Real number system: axioms, completeness, Dedekind cuts, compact sets
  • Sequences and series of functions: limit, continuity, uniform continuity, differentiation and integration
  • Infinite series: convergence and divergence, power series, Taylor expansion with remainder
  • Metric and topological spaces: limit points, open/closed sets, interior/exterior/boundary points, continuous mapping, Cauchy sequences
  • Measure and integrals: Cramer measurability, measurable functions, Lebesgue measure on real line and plane
  • Riemann-Stieltjes integrals
  • Introduction to R: history, data structures (vectors, lists, data frames), reading/writing data
  • Control structures, functions (built-in and custom), simulation, calling C from R
  • Exploratory data analysis with tidyverse: dplyr, ggplot2, grammar of graphics
  • Applications: optimizing non-linear functions using Newton-Raphson, numerical integration
  • Random number generation: congruential generators, seeding
  • Random variate generation: inversion method, rejection method
  • Monte-Carlo integration: hit-and-miss method, improved Monte-Carlo integration
  • Variance reduction: antithetic sampling, importance sampling, control variates
  • Simulated and repeated sampling for illustrating: fitting probability distributions, sampling distributions, point and interval estimation, hypothesis testing
  • Experimental design problems: CRD, RBD, BIBD, Latin square, split-plot, nested designs
  • Demographic data analysis: fertility and mortality measures, life table calculations
  • Using R for experimental design and demographic data analysis
  • Interpreting statistical results for public policy and planning decision-making
  • Comprehensive oral examination by a committee of selected faculty members
  • Assessment of understanding across all second-year courses
  • Evaluation of ability to connect concepts from sampling, inference, and experimental design
  • Demonstration of computing skills and statistical software proficiency

Third Year (36 Credits)

  • Two-factor factorial design: statistical analysis, model adequacy checking, estimating parameters, sample size, assumption of no interaction
  • General factorial design; fitting response curves and surfaces; blocking in factorial design
  • 2^k factorial design: 2^2, 2^3, general 2^k, single replicate, blocking, confounding in 2^p blocks, partial confounding
  • Two-level fractional factorial designs: one-half and one-quarter fractions, general 2^(k-p), resolution III, IV, and V designs
  • Three-level and mixed-level factorial and fractional factorial designs
  • Response surface methods: method of steepest ascent, second-order response surface, mixture experiments, robust designs
  • Experiments with random factors: random effects model, two-factor factorial with random factors, mixed model, sample size determination
  • Nested and split-plot designs: two-stage nested, m-staged nested, split-plot, split-split-plot, strip-split-plot designs
  • Probability proportional to size (PPS) sampling: with replacement (WR) and without replacement (WOR)
  • Hansen-Hurwitz estimator, Horvitz-Thompson estimator, Brewer's method, Durbin's method, Des Raj method, Murthy's method
  • Sub-sampling and two-stage sampling: design, estimators, variances, optimum sample sizes
  • Double sampling: application in stratified sampling, ratio and regression estimators
  • Complex surveys: variance estimation approaches, replication methods (random group, BRR, Jackknife, Bootstrap)
  • Non-sampling errors: sources, nonresponse effects, Politz-Simmons adjustment, imputation methods
  • Special designs: multiplicity/network sampling, adaptive cluster sampling (ACS)
  • Correlation and inference; simple linear regression: least squares, MLE, inferences on parameters, confidence bands
  • Model accuracy: F-test, R², prediction intervals, residual analysis, outlier detection, lack of fit test
  • Transformations, weighted least squares; matrix representation of regression
  • Multiple linear regression: estimation, inference, extra sum of squares, general linear hypothesis
  • Model diagnostics: checking assumptions, multicollinearity, influential observations
  • Polynomial regression: one and multiple variables, piecewise polynomial fitting, orthogonal polynomials
  • Indicator variables, variable selection, model building criteria
  • Validation of regression models: cross-validation
  • Disease processes, statistical approaches, study design, binary outcome data, causality
  • Measures of disease occurrence: prevalence, incidence, disease rates, hazard function
  • Measures of disease-exposure association: relative risk, odds ratio, relative hazard, attributable risk
  • Study designs: population-based, cohort, case-control, case-cohort studies
  • Confounding and interaction: causal inference, counterfactuals, causal graphs, Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test
  • Multiplicative and additive interaction, tests for trend in risk
  • Matching: types and analysis of matched studies
  • Lifetime distributions: exponential, Weibull, log-normal, log-logistic, gamma, inverse Gaussian, mixture models
  • Censoring and truncation: right censoring, types of incomplete data, likelihood construction
  • Nonparametric estimation: Kaplan-Meier estimator, Nelson-Aalen estimator, life tables
  • Parametric inference: exponential, gamma, inverse Gaussian; grouped and interval-censored data
  • Log-location-scale distributions: Weibull, extreme-value, log-normal, log-logistic inference
  • Model assessment: probability plots, goodness-of-fit tests
  • Approximate methods: likelihood inference, Newton-type optimization, EM algorithm, REML, profile likelihood
  • Bayesian inference: prior and posterior distributions, credible intervals, hypothesis testing, MCMC methods
  • Exact tests: tests for proportions, comparison of two proportions
  • Resampling: bootstrap confidence intervals and tests, jackknife, permutation tests
  • Nonparametric inference: empirical CDF, quantiles, statistical functionals, influence functions, kernel density estimation
  • Random vectors and matrices: mean vectors, covariance matrices, matrix inequalities, generalized variance
  • Multivariate normal distribution: properties, sampling, maximum likelihood estimation, assessing normality
  • Inferences about mean vector: Hotelling's T², likelihood ratio tests, confidence regions, missing data
  • Comparisons of several multivariate means: paired comparisons, one-way MANOVA, two-way MANOVA, profile analysis, growth curves
  • Multivariate linear regression: least squares, inference, model checking, multivariate multiple regression
  • Types of research: descriptive vs. analytical, applied vs. fundamental, quantitative vs. qualitative
  • Problem identification, literature review, research questions, hypotheses formulation
  • Research design: exploratory, descriptive, experimental; measurement, validity, reliability
  • Data collection: FGD, IDI, KII, ethnography, surveys, questionnaire design, data collection apps (SurveyCTO, Kobo Toolbox)
  • Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E): baseline, ongoing, end-line evaluation; relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, impact
  • Data analysis, report writing, scholarly publishing, ethics, plagiarism, reproducibility
  • Experiential learning: research proposal, field work, data analysis, report writing
  • Stata interface: windows, files, help system, data entry, reading different formats
  • Data management: browse, edit, sort, describe, generate, replace, egen, reshape, collapse, weighting
  • Graphs: histograms, scatterplots, line plots, box plots, combining graphs
  • Exploratory analysis: summarize, tabstat, table, frequency tables, cross-tabulations
  • Python fundamentals: syntax, data types, control structures, functions, lists, tuples, sets, dictionaries
  • NumPy: arrays, indexing, slicing, arithmetic and matrix operations
  • pandas: DataFrame, Series, data indexing, handling missing data, combining datasets, aggregation, grouping
  • Data visualization: matplotlib and seaborn (bar plots, histograms, density plots, boxplots)
  • Factorial design analysis, screening experiments, split-plot designs, response surfaces
  • Epidemiological analysis: odds ratio, risk ratio, risk difference estimation and inference
  • Survival analysis: non-parametric and parametric estimation of survival and cumulative hazard functions
  • Regression models: simple and multiple linear regression, inference, diagnostics, model selection
  • Multivariate analysis: exploratory analysis, multivariate tests, MANOVA, multivariate regression
